Indian Singers

Shreya Ghoshal

Shreya Ghoshal is one of India’s most iconic and versatile playback singers. She began her musical journey at a young age and gained fame after winning the reality show Sa Re Ga Ma Pa. She made her Bollywood debut with Sanjay Leela Bhansali’s Devdas in 2002, earning a National Award at just 18. Known for her soulful, expressive voice, she has sung in over 20 Indian languages and received numerous awards, including National Film Awards and Filmfare Awards.

Anirudh Ravichander

Anirudh rejuvenated Indian film music—bringing an electrifying mix of global genres and contemporary beats while maintaining melodic charm. His catchy, youthful scores resonate deeply with today's audiences, making him a definitive voice in South Indian cinema and beyond.Became one of India's highest-paid composers, rumored to command ₹8–10 crore per project.Maintains a vegetarian lifestyle and keeps his personal life relatively private.

K. S. Chithra

K. S. Chithra isn't just a playback singer—she’s a cultural icon whose voice transcends language, genre, and generation. Her ability to blend classical finesse with emotive expression has made her a pillar of Indian music, especially in South Indian cinema.Musical upbringing under her father, followed by Carnatic training from Prof. K. Omanakutty; topped her university in music. Deeply spiritual, known for devotional songs; now mentoring emerging talents via her label Audiotracs.

Arijit Singh

Arijit Singh’s soulful voice, versatility, and streaming dominance have cemented him as arguably the defining voice of 21st-century Indian music. With global recognition, record-breaking concerts, and innovative collaborations, his influence continues to expand.Named most-streamed Indian artist from 2020–2024; most-followed artist globally with over 150 million followers.
